What is a Residual?

A residual is the difference between an observed value and the value predicted by a statistical model. In regression analysis, residuals help us understand how well our model fits the data and identify potential outliers or patterns.

Basic Residual Formula:

$$\text{Residual} = \text{Observed Value} - \text{Predicted Value}$$

$$e_i = y_i - \hat{y}_i$$

Residuals are crucial for:

  • Model validation and diagnostics
  • Identifying outliers and influential points
  • Checking model assumptions
  • Improving model performance
